History

The Seven Wonders of the Ancient World

The Seven wonders of the Ancient World are based of the Greek historian Herodotus that wrote about seven astonishing architectural achievements. This writings were to become the list of the Seven wonders of the Ancient World.

  • Great Pyramid of Giza, Egypt
  • Lighthouse of Alexandria, Egypt
  • Mausoleum at Halicarnassus, Turkey
  • Colossus of Rhodes, Greece
  • Temple of Artemis, Turkey
  • Statue of Zeus, Greece
  • Hanging Gardens of Babylon, Iraq

Unfortunately, six out of this seven wonders were destroyed by natural disasters such as earthquakes or fires. The only one still standing is the great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of giza was built in 2570BC and is still standing. It was built as a tomb for the Egyptian pharaoh Khufu and was a part of a complex that included temples and pyramids. Initially standing at 146.6 metres it was the world's tallest human-made structure for more than 3,800 years, but over time the pyramid's height was reduced to the current 138.5 metres.

Why exactly seven Wonders?

You might think with such a large of amazing monuments why not all of them become Wonders of the World. The reason of why there are only seven wonders in because the ancient Greeks believed in the number seven as a number that represented parfection, thats why Herodotus wrote only about seven architectural achievements.
